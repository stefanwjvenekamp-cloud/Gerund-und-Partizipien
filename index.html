Gerund

A gerund is the -ing form of a verb used like a noun. It names an activity or idea: Reading improves your vocabulary.

You often use it after prepositions and certain verbs: interested in learning, avoid repeating, enjoy discussing.

Present participle

A present participle is also an -ing form, but it does not work like a noun. It adds information about an action, a reason, or a description.

It can make sentences smoother: Looking at the data, she changed her opinion. The person after the comma must do the action.

Past participle

A past participle is often the -ed form or the third verb form, such as written, seen, made. It usually shows that someone or something receives an action.

It is useful for concise academic style: Written clearly, the essay is easy to follow. The essay was written; it did not write itself.
