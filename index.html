Gerund

Noun-like -ing form: subject, object, after prepositions and set expressions. Example: Reading improves style.

Present participle

Verb/adjective-like -ing form: reduced clauses, simultaneous actions, descriptions. Example: Walking home, she revised.

Past participle

-ed/third form for passive or completed meaning. Example: Written clearly, the essay convinced the reader.
